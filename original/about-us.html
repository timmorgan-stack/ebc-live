The Studio

Acoustic Design by the Legendary Roger D’Arcy

Every inch of EBC has been acoustically treated by the renowned architect Roger D’Arcy, ensuring unparalleled sound quality. Whether you're recording an album, filming a music video, or staging a photoshoot, EBC delivers an exceptional experience with its full blackout facility and impeccable acoustics. Roger D'Arcy is an architect and acoustic designer with over 30 years of experience in the planning and architectural-acoustic design of recording studios. He’s Founder of Recording Architecture (RA), a UK-based firm specialising in studio design and Co-author of "Acoustic Design for the Home Studio" with Hugh Flynn. n addition to his architectural work, Roger D'Arcy is also a singer/songwriter. He has released several solo albums, including "Crooked Tales" (2016), "House of Heads" (2017), and "The Road To Stameen" (2019)

Roger D'Arcy's work in studio design has been widely recognised in the music industry. He has been described as an "international man of studio design" and his firm, Recording Architecture, has gained a reputation for innovative and high-quality studio designs. D'Arcy has designed studios around the world, with projects in over 40 countries.

Some notable studios designed by D'Arcy and his firm include:

  • Lansdowne Studios

  • CTS Studios

  • Installations for Abbey Road

  • Studios for EMI Music

  • Crystalphonic

  • Optimum Mastering

Welcome to Empire Broadcasting Corporation (EBC Studio) – East London's Premier Recording Studio

EBC Music Studio positions itself as a premier destination for high-quality music production and recording, offering a unique combination of cutting-edge technology and artistic ambiance. The studio is designed to cater to a wide range of musical needs, from large orchestral recordings to intimate solo sessions, created by Marlon Brown and the exceptional acoustic design by Roger D’Arcy.

Originally a historic Methodist church, this building has been transformed into a state-of-the-art, 1,500-square-foot soundproof live room. It features a dramatic 30-foot-high pine vaulted ceiling and a unique surrounding balcony.
The Art of Marlon Alexander Brown

Marlon is a contemporary multi-disciplinary artist whose work delves deep into the realms of surrealism, social commentary, and existential exploration. His creations invite viewers to ponder the complexities of modern life, spirituality, and human nature. His work also reflects an acute awareness of contemporary issues—ranging from economic disparities to environmental concerns—often conveyed through satirical depictions or allegorical scenarios. This approach not only critiques but also encourages reflection on our collective responsibilities as global citizens.

Performance Art

His performative acts blur the lines between art and activism. By holding signs with provocative messages like 'THE BEGINNING IS NIGH' or 'THOU SHALT NOT BANK,' his art enters public spaces and sparks dialogue among audiences. These performances are integral to understanding his oeuvre, serving as living extensions of the themes explored in his static works while emphasizing the importance of direct action in effecting change.

Recording Art

Beyond visual arts and performance art, EBC Studio opens up another dimension of soundscapes that complement visual narratives, providing a platform for other artists who share similar visions for social change through creative expression.

EBC serves as a collaborative space for musicians seeking innovative sounds rooted in profound messages about society—and another medium through which boundaries are pushed, nay destroyed entirely, conceptually (and technically).

“This studio is a space to inspire others within the artistic community and beyond towards greater introspection about our ever-evolving society!” Marlon Alexander Brown
